Structured Approaches to AI Development

Dragos Hont
December 2024
Project Portfolio

Abstract

An exploration of modular, checklist-driven approaches to AI and machine learning development, drawing from experience in software engineering and academic research in artificial intelligence.

Type
Preprint

Introduction

This work examines how structured, modular approaches from software engineering can enhance AI development processes. Drawing on 15+ years of experience building robust systems and ongoing academic study in artificial intelligence, it proposes a framework for specification-driven AI development.

Key Concepts

Modular Thinking

Breaking complex AI systems into manageable, testable components that can be developed, validated, and deployed independently.

Checklist-Driven Development

Applying systematic verification approaches to ensure quality, reproducibility, and ethical considerations throughout the AI development lifecycle.

Workflow Mining

Leveraging machine learning to analyze and optimize development workflows, identifying patterns and bottlenecks in the AI delivery pipeline.

Future Directions

This research will continue as part of the MSc AI programme, with planned applications in:

  • MLOps and model deployment
  • AI governance and responsible AI frameworks
  • Requirements engineering for AI systems
  • Automated workflow analysis and optimization
